Fracture and Dislocation of Atlas
The atlas (vertebra C1) is a bony ring, with two wedge-
shaped lateral masses, connected by relatively thin 
anterior and posterior arches and a transverse liga-
ment (Fig. 4.4A). Because the taller side of the lateral mass is 
directed laterally, vertical forces (as would result from striking 
the bottom of a pool in a diving accident) compressing the lat-
eral masses between the occipital condyles and the axis drive 
them apart, fracturing one or both of the anterior or posterior 
arches (Fig. B4.4B).
