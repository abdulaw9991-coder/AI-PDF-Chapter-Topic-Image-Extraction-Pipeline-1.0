PHALANGES
The 14 phalanges of the lower limb are as follows: the 1st 
digit (great toe) has 2 phalanges (proximal and distal); the 
other four digits have 3 phalanges each: proximal, middle, 
and distal (Fig. 5.11A & D). Each phalanx has a base (proxi-
mally), a shaft, and a head (distally). The phalanges of the 
1st digit are short, broad, and strong. The middle and distal 
phalanges of the 5th digit may be fused in elderly people.
