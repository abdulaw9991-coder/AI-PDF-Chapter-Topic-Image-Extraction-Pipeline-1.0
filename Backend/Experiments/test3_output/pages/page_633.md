The nutrient artery of tibia, the largest nutrient artery 
in the body, arises from the origin of the anterior or poste-
rior tibial artery. It pierces the tibialis posterior, to which 
it supplies branches, and enters the nutrient foramen in 
the proximal third of the posterior surface of the tibia (see 
Fig. 5.9).
Surface Anatomy of Leg
The tibial tuberosity is an easily palpable elevation on the 
anterior aspect of the proximal part of the tibia, approxi-
mately 5 cm distal to the apex of the patella (Fig. 5.65A & B). 
This oval elevation indicates the level of the head of the ﬁ
 bula 
and the bifurcation of the popliteal artery into the anterior 
and posterior tibial arteries.
