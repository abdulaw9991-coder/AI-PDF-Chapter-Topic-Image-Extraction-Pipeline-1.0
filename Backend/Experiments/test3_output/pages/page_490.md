Lumbar Spinal Stenosis
Lumbar spinal stenosis describes a stenotic (nar-
row) vertebral foramen in one or more lumbar 
vertebrae (Fig. B4.6B). This condition may be a 
hereditary anomaly that can make a person more vulner-
able to age-related degenerative changes such as IV disc 
bulging. Lumbar spinal nerves increase in size as the ver-
tebral column descends, but paradoxically, the IV foram-
ina decrease in size. Narrowing is usually maximal at the 
level of the IV discs. However, stenosis of a lumbar verte-
bral foramen alone may cause compression of one or 
more spinal nerve roots occupying the inferior vertebral 
canal (Fig. 4.1). Surgical treatment of lumbar stenosis 
may consist of decompressive laminectomy (see the blue 
box “Laminectomy,” p. 457). When IV disc protrusion 
occurs in a patient with spinal stenosis (Fig. B4.6B), it 
further compromises a vertebral canal that is already lim-
ited, as does arthritic proliferation and ligamentous 
degeneration.
