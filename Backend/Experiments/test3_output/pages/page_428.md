A pudendal nerve block is a peripheral nerve block that pro-
vides local anesthesia over the S2–S4 dermatomes (the major-
ity of the perineum) and the inferior quarter of the vagina (C in 
Fig. B3.27). It does not block pain from the superior birth canal 
(uterine cervix and superior vagina), so the mother is able to 
feel uterine contractions. It can be re-administered, but to do 
so may be disruptive and involve the use of a sharp instrument 
in close proximity to the infant’s head. The anatomical basis of 
the administration of a pudendal block is provided in the blue 
box “Pudendal and Ilio-inguinal Nerve Blocks,” p. 433.
