LEFT VENTRICLE
The left ventricle forms the apex of the heart, nearly all its 
left (pulmonary) surface and border, and most of the dia-
phragmatic surface (Figs. 1.52 and 1.57). Because arterial 
pressure is much higher in the systemic than in the pulmo-
nary circulation, the left ventricle performs more work than 
the right ventricle.
