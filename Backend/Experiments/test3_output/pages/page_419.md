ARTERIAL SUPPLY AND VENOUS DRAINAGE 
OF VAGINA
The arteries supplying the superior part of the vagina derive 
from the uterine arteries.The arteries supplying the middle 
and inferior parts of the vagina derive from the vaginal and 
internal pudendal arteries (Figs. 3.18 and 3.41).
The vaginal veins form vaginal venous plexuses along 
the sides of the vagina and within the vaginal mucosa 
(Fig. 3.41). These veins are continuous with the uterine 
venous plexus as the uterovaginal venous plexus, and 
drain into the internal iliac veins through the uterine vein. 
This plexus also communicates with the vesical and rectal 
venous plexuses.
