The quadriceps is the great extensor of the leg. Concen-

gravity is important during rising from sitting or squatting, 
climbing and walking up stairs, and for acceleration and pro-
jection (running and jumping) when it is lifting or moving the 
body’s weight. Consequently, it may be three times stronger 
than its antagonistic muscle group, the hamstrings.
