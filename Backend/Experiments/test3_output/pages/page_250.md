A peritoneal fold is a reﬂ
 ection of peritoneum that is 
raised from the body wall by underlying blood vessels, ducts, 
and ligaments formed by obliterated fetal vessels (e.g., the 
umbilical folds on the internal surface of the anterolateral 
abdominal wall, Fig. 2.13). Some peritoneal folds contain 
blood vessels and bleed if cut, such as the lateral umbilical 
folds, which contain the inferior epigastric arteries.
