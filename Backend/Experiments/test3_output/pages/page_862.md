ANTERIOR CRANIAL FOSSA
The inferior and anterior parts of the frontal lobes of the brain 
occupy the anterior cranial fossa, the shallowest of the three 
cranial fossae (Fig. 7.12B). The fossa is formed by the frontal 
bone anteriorly, the ethmoid bone in the middle, and the body 
and lesser wings of the sphenoid posteriorly. The greater part of 
the fossa is formed by the orbital parts of the frontal bone, 
which support the frontal lobes of the brain and form the roofs of 
the orbits. This surface shows sinuous impressions (brain mark-
ings) of the orbital gyri (ridges) of the frontal lobes (Fig. 7.11).
