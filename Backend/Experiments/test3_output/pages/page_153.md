Aspiration of Foreign Bodies
Because the right main bronchus is wider and 
shorter and runs more vertically than the left main 
bronchus, aspirated foreign bodies or food is more 
likely to enter and lodge in it or one of its branches. A poten-
tial hazard encountered by dentists is an aspirated foreign 
body, such as a piece of tooth or ﬁ
 lling material, that is likely 
to enter the right main bronchus. To maintain a more sterile 
environment and avoid aspiration of foreign objects, some 
dentists insert a thin rubber dam into the oral cavity before 
performing certain procedures.
