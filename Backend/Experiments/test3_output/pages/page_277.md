CECUM AND APPENDIX
The cecum is the ﬁ
 rst part of the large intestine; it is con-
tinuous with the ascending colon. The cecum is a blind 
intestinal pouch, approximately 7.5 cm in both length and 
breadth. It lies in the iliac fossa of the right lower quadrant 
of the abdomen, inferior to the junction of the terminal ileum 
and cecum (Figs. 2.52 and 2.53). If distended with feces or 
gas, the cecum may be palpable through the anterolateral 
abdominal wall.

lifted freely. However, the cecum has no mesentery. Because 
of its relative freedom, it may be displaced from the iliac fossa,
