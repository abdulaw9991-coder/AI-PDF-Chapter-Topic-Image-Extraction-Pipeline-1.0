Another common site for impaction of gallstones is in 
an abnormal sacculation (Hartmann pouch), that appears 
in diseased states at the junction of the neck of the gall-
bladder and the cystic duct. When this pouch is large, the 
cystic duct arises from its upper left aspect, not from what 
appears to be the apex of the gallbladder. Gallstones com-
monly collect in the pouch. If a peptic duodenal ulcer rup-
tures, a false passage may form between the pouch and the 
superior part of the duodenum, allowing gallstones to enter 
the duodenum. (See the following blue box, “Gallstones in 
Duodenum.”)
