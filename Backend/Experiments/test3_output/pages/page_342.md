QUADRATUS LUMBORUM
The quadrilateral quadratus lumborum forms a thick mus-
cular sheet in the posterior abdominal wall (Figs. 2.94A & 
B, 2.96, and 2.97). It lies adjacent to the lumbar transverse 
processes and is broader inferiorly. Close to the 12th rib, the 
lateral arcuate ligament crosses the quadratus lumborum. 
The subcostal nerve passes posterior to this ligament and 
runs infero 
laterally on the quadratus lumborum. Branches 
of the lumbar plexus run inferiorly on the anterior surface 
of this muscle.
