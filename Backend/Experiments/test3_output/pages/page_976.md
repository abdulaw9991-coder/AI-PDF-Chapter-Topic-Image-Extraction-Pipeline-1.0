ORAL REGION
Cleft Lip
Cleft lip (harelip, a misnomer) is a birth defect (usu-
ally of the upper lip) that occurs in 1 of 1000 births; 
60–80% of affected infants are males. The clefts 
vary from a small notch in the transitional zone of the lip and 
vermilion border to a notch that extends through the lip into 
the nose (Fig. B7.32). In severe cases, the cleft extends 
deeper and is continuous with a cleft in the palate. Cleft lip 
may be unilateral or bilateral (Moore et al., 2012).
