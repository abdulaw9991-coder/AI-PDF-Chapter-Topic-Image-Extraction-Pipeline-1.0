Bones of Forearm
The two forearm bones serve together to form the second unit 
of an articulated mobile strut (the ﬁ
 rst unit being the humerus), 
with a mobile base formed by the shoulder, that positions the 
hand. However, because this unit is formed by two parallel 
bones, one of which (the radius) can pivot about the other (the 
ulna), supination and pronation are possible. This makes it pos-
sible to rotate the hand when the elbow is ﬂ
 exed.
