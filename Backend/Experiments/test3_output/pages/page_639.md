FOOT
The clinical importance of the foot is indicated by the consid-
erable amount of time primary care physicians devote to foot 
problems. Podiatry is the specialized ﬁ
 eld that deals with the 
study and care of the feet.
The ankle refers to the narrowest and malleolar parts 
of the distal leg, proximal to the dorsum and heel of the 
foot, including the ankle joint. The foot, distal to the ankle, 
 
provides a platform for supporting the body when standing 
and has an important role in locomotion.
The skeleton of the foot consists of 7 tarsals, 5 meta 
tarsals,
and 14 phalanges (Fig. 5.66). The foot and its bones may be 
considered in terms of three anatomical and functional zones 
(see Fig. 5.11C, p. 523):
• The hindfoot: talus and calcaneus.
• The midfoot: navicular, cuboid, and cuneiforms.
• The forefoot: metatarsals and phalanges.
