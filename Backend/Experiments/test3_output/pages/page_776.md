containing the muscles in functional groups, are demarcated 
by the subcutaneous border of the ulna posteriorly (in the 
proximal forearm) and then medially (distal forearm) and by 
the radial artery anteriorly and then laterally. These struc-
tures are palpable (the artery by its pulsations) throughout 
the forearm. Because neither boundary is crossed by motor 
nerves, they also provide sites for surgical incision.
The ﬂ

nerve; the one and a half exceptions are innervated by the 
ulnar nerve. The extensors and supinators of the fore-
arm are in the posterior compartment and are all served by 
the radial nerve (directly or by its deep branch).
The fascial compartments of the limbs generally end at 
the joints; therefore, ﬂ

are usually contained and cannot readily spread to other 
compartments. The anterior compartment is exceptional in 
this regard because it communicates with the central com-
