FIGURE 5.11. Bones of right foot. A, B, D, and E. Bones of foot in four views, demonstrating articular surfaces and major prominences and grooves. 
C. The seven bones of the tarsus make up the posterior half of the foot. The talus and calcaneus occupy the posterior two thirds of the tarsus, or the hind-
foot, and the cuboid, navicular, and medial, lateral, and intermediate cuneiforms occupy the anterior third, or midfoot. The metatarsus connects the tarsus 
posteriorly with the phalanges anteriorly. Together, the metatarsus and phalanges make up the anterior half of the foot (forefoot).
