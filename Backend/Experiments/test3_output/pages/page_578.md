Medial Thigh Muscles

prise the adductor group, consisting of the adductor longus, 
adductor brevis, adductor magnus, gracilis, and obturator

Rectus Femoris. The rectus femoris received its name 
because it runs straight down the thigh (L. rectus, straight). 
Because of its attachments to the hip bone and tibia via the 
patellar ligament (Fig. 5.21E & F), it crosses two joints; hence 
it is capable of ﬂ
 exing the thigh at the hip joint and extending 
the leg at the knee joint. The rectus femoris is the only part of 
the quadriceps that crosses the hip joint, and as a hip ﬂ

acts with and like the iliopsoas during the preswing and initial 
swing phases of walking (Fig. 5.20F; Table 5.2).

compromised during hip ﬂ

the extension force during the toe off phase of walking, when
