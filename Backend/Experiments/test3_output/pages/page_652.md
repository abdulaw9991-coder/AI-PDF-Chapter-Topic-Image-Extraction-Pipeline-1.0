The tendons in the ankle region can be identiﬁ

inverted, the tendon of the tibialis posterior may be palpated 
as it passes posterior and distal to the medial malleolus, then 
superior to the sustentaculum tali, to reach its attachment 
to the tuberosity of the navicular (Fig. 5.76A–C). Hence the 
tibialis posterior tendon is the guide to the navicular. The 
tendon of the tibialis posterior also indicates the site for pal-
pating the posterior tibial pulse (halfway between the medial 
malleolus and the calcaneal tendon—Fig. B5.25).
