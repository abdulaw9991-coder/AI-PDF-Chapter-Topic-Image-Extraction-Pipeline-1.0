Fractures of Metatarsals
Metatarsal fractures occur when a heavy object 
falls on the foot, for example, or when it is run over 
by a heavy object such as a metal wheel 
(Fig. B5.8C & D). Metatarsal fractures are also common in 
dancers, especially female ballet dancers who use the 
 
demi-pointe technique. The dancer’s fracture usually 
occurs when the dancer loses balance, putting the full body 
weight on the metatarsal and fracturing the bone. Fatigue 
fractures of the metatarsals may result from prolonged 
walking. These fractures, usually transverse, result from 
repeated stress on the metatarsals.
