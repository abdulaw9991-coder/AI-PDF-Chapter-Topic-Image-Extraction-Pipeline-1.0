PENIS
The penis is the male copulatory organ and, by convey-
ing the urethra, provides the common outlet for urine and 
semen (Figs. 3.60–3.62). The penis consists of a root, body, 
and glans. It is composed of three cylindrical cavernous bod-
ies of erectile tissue: the paired corpora cavernosa dorsally 
and the single corpus spongiosum ventrally. In the ana-
tomical position, the penis is erect; when the penis is ﬂ

corpora is separated (usually incompletely) by the septum 
penis (Fig. 3.61C).
