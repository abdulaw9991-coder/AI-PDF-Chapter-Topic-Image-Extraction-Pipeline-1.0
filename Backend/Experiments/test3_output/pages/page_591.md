Saphenous Varix
A localized dilation of the terminal part of the great 
saphenous vein, called a saphenous varix (L. dilated 
vein), may cause edema in the femoral triangle. A 
saphenous varix may be confused with other groin swellings, 
such as a psoas abscess; however, a varix should be consid-
ered when varicose veins are present in other parts of the 
lower limb.
