Development of Retina
The retina and optic nerve develop from the optic 
cup, an outgrowth of the embryonic forebrain, the 
optic vesicle (Fig. B7.24A). As it evaginates from 
the forebrain (Fig. B7.24B), the optic vesicle carries the 
developing meninges with it. Hence the optic nerve is 
invested with cranial meninges and an extension of the sub-
arachnoid space (Fig. B7.24C). The central artery and vein 
of the retina cross the subarachnoid space and run within the 
distal part of the optic nerve. The pigment cell layer of the 
retina develops from the outer layer of the optic cup, and the 
neural layer develops from the inner layer of the cup.
