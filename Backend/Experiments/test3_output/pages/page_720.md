The cephalic vein (G. kephalé, head) ascends in the 
subcutaneous tissue from the lateral aspect of the dorsal 
venous network, proceeding along the lateral border of the 
wrist and the anterolateral surface of the proximal forearm 
and arm; it is often visible through the skin. Anterior to the 
elbow, the cephalic vein communicates with the median 
cubital vein, which passes obliquely across the anterior 
aspect of the elbow in the cubital fossa (a depression in 
front of the elbow), and joins the basilic vein. The cephalic 
vein courses superiorly between the deltoid and pectora-
lis major muscles along the deltopectoral groove, then 
enters the clavipectoral tri 
angle (Figs. 6.2 and 6.15B).
