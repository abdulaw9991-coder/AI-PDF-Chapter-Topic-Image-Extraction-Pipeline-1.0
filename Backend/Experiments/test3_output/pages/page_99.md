FIGURE I.53. Technique for producing a CT scan. The X-ray tube rotates around the person in the CT scanner and sends a fan-shaped beam of X-rays 
through the upper abdomen from a variety of angles. X-ray detectors on the opposite side of the body measure the amount of radiation that passes through 
a horizontal section. A computer reconstructs the images from several scans, and a CT scan is produced. The scan is oriented so it appears the way an exam-
iner would view it when standing at the foot of the bed and looking toward a supine person’s head.
