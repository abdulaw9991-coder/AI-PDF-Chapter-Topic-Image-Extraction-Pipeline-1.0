Weight transfer at the hip joint is accomplished primarily by the disposition of the bones, with the ligaments limiting the range of movement and adding sta-
bility. B. Articulating surfaces of hip joint and sites of attachment and tendinous relationships of iliofemoral ligaments and joint capsule. C. Iliofemoral liga-
ment. D. The ischiofemoral ligament. Because the joint capsule does not attach to the posterior aspect of the femur, the synovial membrane protrudes from 
the joint capsule, forming the obturator externus bursa to facilitate movement of the tendon of the obturator externus (shown in part C) over the bone.
