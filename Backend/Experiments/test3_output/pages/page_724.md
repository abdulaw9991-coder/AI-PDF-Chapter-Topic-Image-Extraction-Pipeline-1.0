FIGURE 6.18. Segmental (dermatomal) and peripheral (cutaneous nerve) innervation of upper limb. A and B. The pattern of segmental (dermatomal) 
innervation of the upper limb proposed by Foerster (1933) depicts innervation of the medial aspect of the limb by upper thoracic (T1–T3) spinal cord 
segments, consistent with the experience of heart pain (angina pectoris) referred to that area. C and D. The pattern of segmental innervation proposed by

providing a way to approximate the segmental innervation.
