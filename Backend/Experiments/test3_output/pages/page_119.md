(green) lie inside the thoracic cage lateral to the sternum.

FIGURE 1.14. Posterior aspect of anterior thoracic wall. The internal thoracic arteries arise from the subclavian arteries and have paired accompany-
ing veins (L. venae comitantes) inferiorly. Superior to the 2nd costal cartilage, there is only a single internal thoracic vein on each side, which drains into the 
brachiocephalic vein. The continuity of the transversus thoracis muscle with the transversus abdominis muscle becomes apparent when the diaphragm is 
removed, as has been done here on the right side.
