MALE UROGENITAL TRIANGLE
Urethral Catheterization
Urethral catheterization is done to remove urine 
from a person who is unable to micturate. It is also 
performed to irrigate the bladder and to obtain an 
uncontaminated sample of urine. When inserting catheters 
and urethral sounds (slightly conical instruments for explor-
ing and dilating a constricted urethra), the curves of the male 
urethra must be considered. Just distal to the perineal mem-
brane, the spongy urethra is well covered inferiorly and pos-
teriorly by erectile tissue of the bulb of the penis; however, a 
short segment of the intermediate part of the urethra is 
unprotected (Fig. B3.32). Because the urethral wall is thin 
and the angle that must be negotiated to enter the intermedi-
ate part of the spongy urethra, the wall is vulnerable to rup-
ture during the insertion of urethral catheters and sounds.
