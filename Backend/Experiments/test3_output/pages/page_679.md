The main movements of the ankle joint are dorsiﬂ
 exion and 
plantarﬂ

axis passing through the talus (Fig. 5.99B). Because the nar-
row end of the trochlea of the talus lies loosely between the 
malleoli when the foot is plantarﬂ
 exed, some “wobble” (small 
amounts of abduction, adduction, inversion, and eversion) is 
possible in this unstable position.

toe dancing by ballet dancers, for example, the dorsum of
