Contraction of the knee extensors is maintained through 
the heel strike into the loading phase to absorb shock and 
keep the knee from buckling until it reaches full extension. 
Because the unsupported side of the hip tends to drop dur-

phase (Fig. 5.20F & G), pulling on the ﬁ

Of course, all these actions alternate from side to side with 
each step. The extensors of the hip normally make only minor 
contributions to level walking. Primarily, the hip is passively 
extended by momentum during stance, except when accel-
erating or walking fast, and becomes increasingly active with 
increase in slope (steepness) during walking uphill or up stairs.
