The subcostal muscles are variable in size and shape, usu-
ally being well developed only in the lower thoracic wall. 
These thin muscular slips extend from the internal surface of 
the angle of one rib to the internal surface of the second or 
third rib inferior to it. Crossing one or two intercostal spaces, 
the subcostals run in the same direction as the internal inter-
costals and blend with them (Fig. 1.15B).
